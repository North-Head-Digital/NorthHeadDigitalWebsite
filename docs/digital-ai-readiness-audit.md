# Digital & AI Readiness Audit

**30-Minute Free Consultation Template for Local Businesses**

**Purpose:** Identify where a local business is losing time, leads, bookings, reviews,
or revenue because of weak digital systems, outdated websites, poor local search
visibility, or repetitive manual work.

**Ideal clients:** Restaurants, cafes, hotels, inns, retail shops, contractors, cleaners,
property services, salons, attractions, and other brick-and-mortar businesses.

## 3. Opening Script

Use simple language.

> "The goal today is not to sell you technology. I want to understand how customers find
> you, where you lose time, and where you may be missing easy business. At the end, I'll
> summarize the top 2–3 areas where a website, Google cleanup, or simple automation could help."

Then ask: _"Does that sound useful?"_

## 4. Section 1: Business Snapshot

**Goal:** Understand what the business sells, who the customer is, and what kind of result
matters most.

### Questions to ask

**General business**

1. "What are your main products or services?"
2. "Who is your best customer?"
3. "Are most customers local, tourists, second-home owners, or a mix?"
4. "Is your business seasonal, steady year-round, or event-driven?"
5. "What months are busiest?"
6. "What months are slowest?"
7. "What is the one thing you wish more customers knew about your business?"

**Revenue priorities**

8. "What would help you most right now: more calls, more walk-ins, more bookings, better reviews, better-quality leads, or less admin work?"
9. "What type of customer or job is most profitable for you?"
10. "What type of customer or job do you wish you got less of?"

### What to listen for

| Owner says                                     | Opportunity                                     |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| "People do not know we offer that."            | Website copy / Google profile / local SEO       |
| "We are slammed in summer but slow in winter." | Seasonal campaigns / email list / landing pages |
| "We get a lot of bad-fit calls."               | Quote intake / lead qualification               |
| "Everyone asks the same questions."            | FAQ page / AI assistant / automated replies     |
| "We depend on Facebook."                       | Website / Google / email list                   |

## 5. Section 2: Customer Acquisition and Missed Revenue

**Goal:** Find where potential customers are being lost.

### Questions to ask

**How customers find them**

1. "How do most new customers find you?"
2. "Google, Facebook, word of mouth, signs, repeat customers, hotels, referrals, or something else?"
3. "Do you know what people usually search for when they find you?"
4. "Do you show up when someone searches for your service nearby?"
5. "Do you get more calls, website visits, messages, or walk-ins?"

**Missed calls and messages**

6. "How many calls do you think you miss in a normal week?"
7. "During busy season, how many calls do you miss in a day?"
8. "What happens when someone calls and no one answers?"
9. "Do missed calls get called back?"
10. "How fast do you usually respond to voicemails, Facebook messages, emails, or quote requests?"

**Lost leads**

11. "Have you ever had someone say, 'I tried calling but couldn't reach anyone'?"
12. "Do customers usually contact multiple businesses before choosing?"
13. "Do you know how many people fill out your contact form but never become customers?"
14. "Do you follow up with people who asked a question but did not book or buy?"

### Bottleneck indicators

| Bottleneck        | Evidence                                      |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Missed calls      | Owner is too busy to answer                   |
| Slow replies      | Messages sit for hours or days                |
| No follow-up      | Leads disappear after first contact           |
| No tracking       | Owner does not know where leads come from     |
| Bad-fit inquiries | Owner spends time answering unqualified leads |

### Paid project this can become

| Problem found        | Paid solution                             |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Missed calls         | Missed call text-back system              |
| Slow replies         | Contact form routing + automated response |
| Repetitive questions | FAQ page or AI website assistant          |
| Poor lead quality    | Quote intake form                         |
| No tracking          | Basic lead dashboard                      |
| Lost follow-up       | Email/SMS follow-up sequence              |

## 6. Section 3: Website and Local SEO Readiness

**Goal:** Determine whether the business is easy to find, trust, and contact online.

### Questions to ask

**Website**

1. "When was your website last updated?"
2. "Can you update it yourself?"
3. "Is there anything on the website that is outdated right now?"
4. "Does your website clearly explain what you sell?"
5. "Does it clearly say who you serve and where?"
6. "Does it work well on a phone?"
7. "Can customers easily call, book, order, or request a quote?"
8. "Do you know how many people visit your website each month?"

**Google visibility**

9. "Do you manage your Google Business Profile?"
10. "Are your hours always correct on Google?"
11. "Do you post updates, photos, specials, or announcements on Google?"
12. "Do you know what category your business is listed under?"
13. "Do you appear on Google Maps for your most important service or product?"

**Reviews**

14. "How often do you ask customers for reviews?"
15. "Do you respond to reviews?"
16. "Do you have a simple review link you send customers?"
17. "Do negative reviews get handled quickly?"
18. "Do your recent reviews mention the things you want to be known for?"

### Local SEO red flags

| Red flag                           | Why it matters                                 |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Website older than 3–5 years       | May look abandoned or perform poorly on mobile |
| No Google Business Profile control | Owner cannot manage local search presence      |
| Wrong hours                        | Lost walk-ins and angry customers              |
| Few recent reviews                 | Lower trust                                    |
| No location/service-area language  | Poor local search relevance                    |
| No clear call-to-action            | Visitors leave without contacting              |
| No analytics                       | Owner cannot see what is working               |

### Paid project this can become

| Problem found          | Paid solution                   |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Outdated website       | Digital Essentials website      |
| Weak Google profile    | Google Business Profile cleanup |
| Poor mobile experience | Mobile-first redesign           |
| No local pages         | Local SEO service pages         |
| Few reviews            | Review request automation       |
| No tracking            | Analytics dashboard             |
| Weak event visibility  | Seasonal/event landing pages    |

## 7. Section 4: Operational Bottlenecks

**Goal:** Find repetitive manual tasks that can become automation or AI consulting projects.

### Questions to ask

**Repetitive customer questions**

1. "What questions do customers ask over and over?"
2. "Do people ask about hours, pricing, availability, menus, policies, directions, parking, or service area?"
3. "Do staff answer these questions the same way every time?"
4. "Are the answers written down anywhere?"

**Scheduling and booking**

5. "How do customers book, reserve, or request service?"
6. "Is scheduling handled by phone, text, paper, Facebook, email, or software?"
7. "Do customers ever fall through the cracks?"
8. "Do you send reminders?"
9. "Do you have no-shows or last-minute cancellations?"

**Invoicing and payment**

10. "How do you send invoices?"
11. "Are invoices created manually?"
12. "Do you have to chase people for payment?"
13. "Do you send payment reminders?"
14. "Do customers ask for receipts, estimates, or job details repeatedly?"

**Internal operations**

15. "What tasks do you repeat every week that feel like a waste of time?"
16. "What information lives mostly in your head?"
17. "If you were gone for a week, what would staff struggle with?"
18. "Do you have written checklists, policies, or standard responses?"
19. "Do seasonal employees need to be trained on the same things repeatedly?"

**Contractor-specific questions**

20. "How do you collect job details before giving a quote?"
21. "Do customers send photos?"
22. "Do you ask for address, urgency, budget, and project type?"
23. "How often do you drive out for jobs that are not worth quoting?"
24. "Do you know which types of jobs are most profitable?"

**Restaurant/hospitality-specific questions**

25. "How often do menus, specials, rates, or availability change?"
26. "Where do you have to update that information?"
27. "Do guests ask the same check-in, pet, parking, or beach-access questions?"
28. "Do you collect guest emails for future promotions?"
29. "Do you follow up after a visit?"

### Paid project this can become

| Bottleneck                 | Web/AI project              |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Same questions all day     | FAQ page + AI assistant     |
| Missed calls               | Text-back automation        |
| Manual quotes              | Quote intake form           |
| Manual invoices            | Invoice workflow setup      |
| Poor payment follow-up     | Payment reminder automation |
| Staff ask owner everything | Internal knowledge base     |
| No customer list           | Email/SMS capture system    |
| Outdated menus/specials    | Menu update workflow        |
| No review habit            | Review request system       |
| Poor seasonal reactivation | Email campaign system       |

## 8. Section 5: AI Comfort and Risk Tolerance

**Goal:** Understand how skeptical the owner is and avoid over-positioning AI.

### Questions to ask

1. "Have you used ChatGPT or any AI tool before?"
2. "What concerns you about AI?"
3. "Would you be comfortable with AI drafting responses if you approve them first?"
4. "Would you be comfortable with an AI assistant answering only basic website questions?"
5. "Are there things you would never want automated?"
6. "Would you prefer automation that drafts things for review, or automation that sends simple messages automatically?"
7. "Who on your team would use these tools?"

### Owner concern translation

| Owner concern                                 | Response                                                                        |
| --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "I do not want a robot talking to customers." | "Then we keep it limited to drafts or basic approved answers."                  |
| "I do not trust AI."                          | "We only use it where the answer is based on your approved information."        |
| "My customers want a real person."            | "This helps people reach the real person faster."                               |
| "I am not technical."                         | "You do not need to manage the tool. You approve the rules and we maintain it." |
| "I do not want to replace staff."             | "This is for missed calls, repeat questions, and after-hours support."          |

## 10. The "Bottleneck-to-Project" Proposal Framework

Use this structure after the consultation.

**Step 1: Name the business goal.** Examples:

- "Get more calls from Google."
- "Recover missed leads."
- "Reduce repetitive customer questions."
- "Make the business easier to find on mobile."
- "Turn one-time visitors into repeat customers."
- "Improve quote quality before spending time on calls."

**Step 2: Identify the bottleneck.** Use plain language.

> "Right now, customers are calling during busy hours, but if no one answers, there is no
> automatic follow-up. That means some people move on to the next business."

**Step 3: Connect the bottleneck to lost money or wasted time.**

> "Even two lost calls per week could matter if those calls are reservations, service jobs,
> or larger purchases."

**Step 4: Recommend the simplest paid project.**

> "The first project I would recommend is a missed-call text-back system and a cleaner
> contact page. That gives people an immediate response and gives you a better chance to
> save the lead."

**Step 5: Offer 2 options, not 5.** Avoid overwhelming the owner.

| Option        | Best for                                | Price  |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------ |
| Basic fix     | Solve the immediate problem             | $995   |
| Better system | Solve the problem and improve follow-up | $3,500 |

**Step 6: End with a clear next step.**

> "The next step would be a short paid setup project. I would clean up the website contact
> flow, set up the missed-call response, and give you a simple dashboard so you can see what
> is coming in."

## 11. Proposal Format

Keep the proposal to 1–2 pages for local owners.

**1. Current situation**

> "Your business gets most inquiries through phone calls and Google. Your website is
> functional but does not make it easy for mobile visitors to quickly request service. You
> also miss calls during busy periods, and there is no automatic follow-up."

**2. Main bottlenecks** (use 3 bullets maximum)

- Missed calls are not being recovered.
- Google profile is not being actively updated.
- Quote requests do not collect enough information upfront.

**3. Recommended solution**

> "I recommend the Growth & Automation package with a focus on missed-call recovery, Google
> cleanup, and a quote intake form."

**4. Deliverables**

- Website contact page cleanup
- Google Business Profile optimization
- Missed-call text-back setup
- Quote request form
- Review request link
- Basic analytics tracking
- Owner handoff and training

**5. Timeline**

> "Estimated build time: 2–4 weeks depending on content and approvals."

**6. Investment**

> Project setup: $3,500
> Optional monthly support: $250/month

**7. Expected outcome** (use practical language, not guarantees)

> "The goal is to make it easier for customers to reach you, reduce wasted back-and-forth,
> and recover more inquiries that would otherwise be missed."

## 12. Consultation Script by Business Type

### For a restaurant

Ask:

1. "How often do customers call just to ask if you are open?"
2. "Is your menu current online?"
3. "Are specials posted anywhere besides Facebook?"
4. "Do customers ask about wait times, reservations, dietary options, or takeout?"
5. "Do you collect emails or phone numbers from regulars or visitors?"
6. "Do you ask happy customers for reviews?"

Likely projects: Menu refresh system, Google profile cleanup, review request system,
AI FAQ assistant, seasonal campaign, mobile website refresh.

### For a hotel, inn, or vacation rental business

Ask:

1. "How do most guests book?"
2. "Do you prefer direct bookings or third-party bookings?"
3. "What questions do guests ask before arrival?"
4. "What questions do guests ask during their stay?"
5. "Do you collect guest emails for repeat visits?"
6. "Do you promote event weekends or off-season specials?"

Likely projects: Direct booking website improvements, guest FAQ assistant, email
reactivation system, event landing pages, review automation, local guide page.

### For a retail shop

Ask:

1. "Do tourists buy from you again after they leave?"
2. "Do you sell gift cards?"
3. "Do you have best-selling items that could be sold online?"
4. "Do customers know what makes your shop different?"
5. "Do you post new items or seasonal products?"
6. "Do you collect customer emails?"

Likely projects: Google profile cleanup, simple website refresh, gift card/e-commerce
starter, email list setup, seasonal content calendar, review request system.

### For a contractor or service business

Ask:

1. "How do people usually request quotes?"
2. "Do they give you enough information upfront?"
3. "How many calls are bad-fit jobs?"
4. "Do customers send photos?"
5. "Do you know which jobs are most profitable?"
6. "Do you have recent project photos and reviews online?"

Likely projects: Quote intake form, service-area landing pages, before/after portfolio,
Google profile cleanup, review system, missed-call text-back.

## 13. Red Flags That Justify a Paid Project

### Immediate Tier 1 opportunities

- No website
- Website is clearly outdated
- Website is not mobile-friendly
- Google hours are wrong
- No review link
- Contact info is hard to find
- Business depends only on Facebook

### Immediate Tier 2 opportunities

- Missed calls are common
- Owner answers the same questions daily
- Quote requests are messy
- Business has poor follow-up
- Reviews are inconsistent
- Website exists but does not convert
- Business is busy but operationally strained

### Tier 3 opportunities

- New business launch
- New location
- Major rebrand
- New restaurant/lodging concept
- New contractor business
- Business has no digital foundation
- Owner wants website, Google, booking, content, and automation together

or:

## 16. Package Recommendation Logic

### Recommend Digital Essentials when:

- They need the basics fixed.
- Budget sensitivity is high.
- Website and Google presence are weak.
- They are skeptical of automation.
- The business is small and owner-operated.

Typical proposal: **$750–$1,500 setup + $75–$175/month**

### Recommend Growth & Automation when:

- They already get steady customers.
- They miss calls or messages.
- They waste time on repetitive admin.
- They need better follow-up.
- Their website exists but does not help operations.

Typical proposal: **$2,500–$5,000 setup + $250–$650/month**

### Recommend Startup Launchpad when:

- They are launching from scratch.
- They need strategy, website, Google, content, and systems.
- They are opening a new location or doing a major relaunch.
- The project requires more than a basic website.

Typical proposal: **$5,000–$10,000+ setup + $500–$1,250/month**

## 17. Best Way to Frame AI in the Proposal

Do not make AI the headline.

**Weak framing**

> "We will implement AI automation tools for your business."

**Strong framing**

> "We will set up a simple system that answers common questions, captures missed inquiries,
> and saves your staff time. You approve the information it uses, and complex questions still
> go to a person."

**Stronger local-business framing**

> "This helps customers get basic answers when you are busy, closed, or short-staffed."

## Core Rule for the Audit

The consultation should not feel like a tech diagnosis. It should feel like this:

> "Where are customers getting stuck, and where is the owner wasting time?"

Then the paid proposal becomes obvious:

- Website fixes help customers trust and contact the business.
- Google fixes help customers find the business.
- Automation helps prevent lost leads.
- AI helps reduce repetitive questions.
- Monthly support keeps everything current.
